Stylistic Co-Speech Gesture Generation: Modeling Personality and Communicative Styles in Virtual Agents

Rodolfo Luis Tonoli, Paula Dornhofer Paro Costa
Depto. de Engenharia de Computação e Automação (DCA)
Faculdade de Engenharia Elétrica e de Computação (FEEC)
Universidade Estadual de Campinas (UNICAMP), Brazil.
Code Data

Generate gestures in virtual agenst for unseen audio consistent with the personality and communicative style of the agent.

Abstract

This research introduces a framework designed to model personality traits and communicative styles in speech-driven gestures for virtual agents. We present an expressive gesture dataset synchronized with speech audio in Brazilian Portuguese, featuring three personalities and three communicative styles. We introduce a diffusion-based gesture generation model that captures the nuance of gestures based on speech audio input. Objective and perceptual evaluations are employed to assess the naturalness, human-likeness, and appropriateness of the generated gestures, as well as the recognition accuracy of personality and communicative styles. Results demonstrate that our model achieves similar human-likeness and appropriateness to previous state-of-the-art system while producing more expressive gestures. We also provide insights into the challenges of recognising styles based on gestures alone.

Diffusion-based model

Our model simultaneously generates motion sequences of 120 poses, corresponding to 4 seconds of motion. We employ the pre-trained WavLM and VAD models to extract audio features and take the style as conditional input.
